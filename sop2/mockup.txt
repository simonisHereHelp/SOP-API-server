1. **Standard Deduction Increase**: The standard deduction has been slightly increased to account for inflation. For single filers, the deduction is now $13,850, and for married couples filing jointly, it is $27,700.

2. **Tax Brackets Adjusted for Inflation**: The tax brackets have been adjusted to reflect inflation, meaning the income thresholds for each bracket have increased slightly. 3. **Child Tax Credit**: The Child Tax Credit remains at $2,000 per qualifying child under age 17. However, there are discussions about potential changes, so keep an eye out for any updates.

5. **Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)**: The maximum EITC for 2024 is $7,430 for taxpayers with three or more qualifying children. The credit decreases for those with fewer children.

6. **Education Credits**: The American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC) and Lifetime Learning Credit (LLC) are still available, offering up to $2,500 and $2,000, respectively, for qualifying education expenses.

7. **Health Savings Account (HSA) Contributions**: The HSA contribution limit has increased to $4,150 for individuals and $8,300 for families. An additional $1,000 catch-up contribution is allowed for those aged 55 and older.
